Exploring Indian Cuisines

Discover the Rich Flavors of Indian Food

The remarks of Indian culture had first seen in ancient times but exploded in the 15th century by trading. Besides, during the colonial period, with the lack of labour, a large number of Indian Muslims had migrated to Malaysia with their traditions, culture, and of course food. Explore four distinct Indian cuisines and learn about their signature dishes below.

Banana Leaf Rice

Banana leaf rice is a traditional Malaysian dish that holds great significance in Malaysian culture. It is a unique culinary experience that tantalizes the taste buds and all the senses. This popular dish originated from the South Indian community in Malaysia. It has become a staple in the Malaysian food scene ever since.

Biryani

Biryani came to Singapore and Malaysia from India but the dish originated in Persia (today's Iran).The hearty, flavour and aroma packed meat and rice dish is one of the favourites in Malaysia and Singapore cuisine. The word biryani is derived from two Persian words. Birinj is the Persian word for "rice". The Persian word Birian means "to fry before cooking" which succinctly sums up the basic method of cooking biryani.

Roti Canai

Roti Canai, the beloved Malaysian flatbread, is more than just a staple food but a cultural icon that embodies the rich tapestry of Malaysian cuisine.
Originally from South India, this delectable dish has seamlessly woven its way into the hearts and plates of Malaysians and become an integral part of their culinary heritage.
Roti Canai's journey to becoming Malaysia's national bread is a fascinating tale of cultural fusion and adaptation.
Despite its Indian origins, this menu has evolved into a distinctly Malaysian dish, thanks to the influence of Tamil immigrants who arrived in Malaysia during the colonial era.
Known as Indian Muslims or Mamaks, these immigrants brought with them not just their culinary traditions but also their unique blend of spices, flavors, and cooking techniques.

Chicken Tikka Masala

As well known, curries are many kinds of food that contain a complex combination of spices and herbs, generally prepared in a sauce. Most commonly used ones are coriander, ginger, turmeric, cumin, and of course chiles. These spices can be used either whole or ground and cooked or raw. With the influence of Indian curries, Malaysia had found its own way. Rendang, tamarind, garlic, chilli peppers, ginger, shallots, and coconut milk have become the most typical ingredients for Malaysian curries.
Chicken Tikka Masala in Malaysia is a delightful fusion of Indian and Malay flavours, showcasing the diversity and creativity of Malaysian cuisine. The marriage of aromatic spices, tender chicken, and creamy coconut-based gravy creates an explosion of flavours that will leave your taste buds craving more. Whether you're a fan of Indian cuisine, Malaysian food, or simply an adventurous foodie, exploring the Malaysian rendition of Chicken Tikka Masala is a must-try experience that is sure to delight your senses.
